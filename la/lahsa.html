LAHSA Exposed

Where $800 million a year goes. Every grantee. Every executive salary. Every red flag.
"A noble, logical diagram once recorded will not die." Daniel H. Burnham, 1907

The Los Angeles Homeless Services Authority distributes roughly $800 million in public funds annually to a network of nonprofit contractors. Most Angelenos cannot name a single grantee. This page is the diagram. Each row below represents an organization that received LAHSA contracts. The financials come directly from IRS Form 990 filings via ProPublica's Nonprofit Explorer. Red flags are calculated automatically using the methodology described below.

This is not an allegation of wrongdoing. It is a record. A record that can be checked, disputed, and updated. The first step in accountability is knowing where the money went.

Red Flag Methodology

Rows highlighted in red meet one or more of the following criteria based on 990 data:

  • High surplus ratio: (Revenue − Expenses) / Revenue > 35%. An organization retaining more than 35 cents of every dollar may be accumulating reserves rather than delivering services.
  • Negative revenue entries indicate reporting anomalies or asset writedowns and are flagged for review.
  • Organizations with no 990 data on file with ProPublica are listed as N/A and may warrant direct inquiry to LAHSA.

Source: IRS Form 990 filings retrieved via ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer. Most recent available filing shown. Data reflects the tax year reported, not the LAHSA contract year.
