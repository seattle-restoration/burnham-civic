The Precedent

In 1898, Seattle decided a hill was in the way. So they deleted it. R.H. Thomson moved 11 million cubic yards of earth over 30 years to flatten Denny Hill. Today, Amazon's headquarters sits where the hill used to be.

In 1889, Seattle burned to the ground. Within 25 days, the city council passed the most comprehensive building code in America. Within a year, 465 brick buildings were completed. Pioneer Square is the result.

In 1909, Seattle built the largest man-made island on Earth. They dredged the Duwamish River, filled the tidal flats, and created Harbor Island. No federal funding. No environmental review. Just civic ambition.

The Problem

Seattle's civic infrastructure has been captured. $300+ million in public homelessness funding produces worsening outcomes. Per-unit costs run $400,000+ while Houston solves the same problem at $180,000. Public records requests go unanswered. Accountability is nonexistent.

The city that moved mountains now can't move paperwork.
