The Physical Overhaul

Infrastructure Blitz: What Stays Forever

Camp clearings and patrols are temporary. The physical transformation is permanent. Six specialized crews deploy alongside enforcement - when the feds leave, the improvements remain for decades.

Pothole Emergency Team
72-hour fill program. Every pothole on a priority corridor filled within 3 days of operations starting. Cold patch for immediate fixes, hot asphalt follow-up within 2 weeks. Focus: 3rd Ave, Aurora, SODO approach, stadium corridors, waterfront access roads. Smooth roads signal a functioning city.
Weld & Gate Crew
Every broken gate, railing, and bollard fixed. Welders repair park gates, building entry barriers, fence lines, and sidewalk bollards. Broken infrastructure invites disorder. Repaired infrastructure signals permanence. Priority: parks, public buildings, transit stops, alley barriers.
Door Repair Crew
Public building entries restored. Every broken door, smashed entry panel, boarded window on public-facing buildings repaired or replaced. Coordinate with property owners on commercial frontages. A boarded-up door says "we gave up." A functioning door says "we're open."
Utility Pole Team
Inspect, repair, beautify. Leaning poles straightened. Damaged bases repaired. Illegal postings removed. Uniform street lighting restored - every burned-out bulb replaced on priority corridors. Pole-mounted banners installed with unified Seattle identity. Clean infrastructure overhead matters.
Park Restoration Crew
Dead grass restored. Landscaping renewed. Every park cleared of encampments gets immediate restoration: resod dead areas, replant damaged beds, repair irrigation, replace destroyed playground equipment, repaint benches. Cal Anderson, Ballard Commons, Denny Park, Occidental - restored to the parks they were built to be.
Trim Team
Unified paint theme across the city. Every public-facing trim, railing, bench, bollard, and fixture painted in a cohesive Seattle color scheme. One theme. One identity. When someone walks through the city 5 years from now, they see the mark of the day Seattle chose to be a city again.

The Theme: A unified color palette applied to trim, railings, benches, bollards, pole bases, park fixtures, and public infrastructure citywide. Not a logo - a material signature. A color system that says "this was all done at once, with intention." Pick it once, apply it everywhere, and it brands Seattle's transformation for years.

"This is federal overreach."
The federal government is hosting an international sporting event on American soil with 5 million visitors. It has a Constitutional obligation to protect them. 40 USC 1315 gives DHS authority over federal property. 8 USC 1357 gives CBP authority in the border zone. 33 CFR gives the Coast Guard authority over navigable waters. FIFA's own contract says "safety is the government's responsibility." This isn't overreach. This is the job.
"Courts will block this."
Operations begin and end before litigation resolves. TROs take days, injunctions take weeks, full litigation takes months. The cleanup is done before the courts catch up. And on what grounds? Federal property protection is settled law. NSSE authority is well-established. The border zone has been upheld repeatedly. D.C. cleaned up and no court stopped it.
"This criminalizes homelessness."
Day 3 of the plan creates 200+ shelter beds. LEAD has a 58% recidivism reduction rate. JustCARE places 70% of participants in permanent housing. Services are offered first, every time. But Grants Pass (SCOTUS 2024) says you can enforce camping bans. The Blake Fix criminalizes drug possession. SODA zones target open-air drug markets. This isn't about poverty. This is about law.
